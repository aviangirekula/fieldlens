How it works

A mentor in your pocket, in three steps.

STEP 1

Point or upload

Aim your phone camera at the component, or upload a photo. No app install. It runs in the browser the tech already has open.

STEP 2

Instant safety verdict

FieldLens identifies the part and returns Safe, Caution, or Do Not Touch with the plain reason why, and a highlight box drawn on the component, colored by hazard. When unsure, it chooses the more cautious verdict.

STEP 3

Guided walkthrough

A step by step inspection drill. What to look at, what normal looks like, the common mistake to avoid, and a field tip, with the box moving to each spot.

Safety

Safety is the product, not a feature.

FieldLens is built for someone standing next to lethal voltage on their first week. When it is not certain, it chooses the more cautious verdict. It never tells a new tech to touch, loosen, or probe anything without confirmed lockout, tagout, and zero voltage verification. Every verdict carries a label and an icon, not color alone.

Defensibility

Why this is hard to copy.

A chatbot that names a part is a weekend project. The compounding parts are ours.

Per step look here highlighting

FieldLens does not just describe a step. It anchors a box onto the exact spot in the tech's own photo, colored by hazard (red, amber, green) so the danger reads directly on the equipment. Spatial, in image guidance a generic chat answer cannot reproduce.

A field data flywheel

FAQ

Honest answers.

Do I need to install an app?

No. FieldLens runs in any phone browser. You open a link, point your phone, and go. There is nothing to download and no account required to try it.

Is this augmented reality?

No. Today FieldLens is computer vision applied to a phone photo. It identifies the component and draws a box on it. Registered AR overlays and AR glasses are a long term roadmap item, not the product you use now.

How accurate is it?

On our internal 30 image benchmark, component identification was 80 percent on strict exact match, and zero of eight clearly hazardous parts were ever called safe. That is product evidence on clean web sourced images, not field validated performance yet. Real field photos are the next benchmark.

What equipment does it cover?

It targets common HVAC and electrical components a first year tech meets, such as capacitors, contactors, and panels. Coverage is expanding. Broader equipment support is on the roadmap.

Does it work offline?

Not yet. It currently needs a connection. An offline and low signal mode for mechanical rooms and basements is on the roadmap, alongside an on device model.

What does it cost?

It is free during the open beta. We are pre revenue and focused on getting it right with design partner technicians and shops before pricing anything.

Who built it, and how do I get involved?

A solo founder with a computer vision and ML research background. If you run a shop or train techs, design partners are welcome. Reach out and we will set you up and learn from how your team actually uses it.
