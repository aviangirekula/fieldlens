Safety

Safety is the product, not a feature.

FieldLens is tuned to err toward caution. When the model is unsure, it does not guess in the technician's favor. It pushes the case to Caution or Do-Not-Touch and tells them why. A false alarm costs a few seconds. A missed hazard does not.

FieldLens is a training and inspection aid. It does not replace a qualified supervisor. Always verify with a licensed professional before working on live equipment.

STATUS LEGEND
Do-Not-Touch

A visible hazard, or too uncertain to clear. Stop and get a second opinion.

Caution

Worth a closer look or an extra step before proceeding.

Safe

Nothing visible to flag on this part. Only ever shown when there is real confidence.

FAQ

Straight answers.

Does FieldLens replace a supervisor?+

No, and it never should. It is a second set of eyes that flags visible hazards and walks through a safe order of work. The qualified supervisor still owns every decision. FieldLens just makes sure fewer things get missed before they get there.

What does it run on?+

Any modern phone browser. There is no app to download and no headset to wear. A technician opens a link and points the camera. That is the whole setup.

How accurate is it?+

Honestly, early. Our numbers come from a small internal test set, not the field. The result we trust most is that it has not marked a visibly hazardous part safe in that set. We are tuning it to stay conservative as we test on more real units.

Is it available now?+

It is an early but working prototype, in open beta and pre-revenue. We are recruiting design-partner shops and technicians to test it in the field. If that is you, we want to talk.

Who is it for?+

New HVAC technicians, and the shops training them. It is built for the person on day thirty who is alone at a unit, not for a classroom. The framing is safety and ramp-up, not certification.

Is this AR?+

No. Today FieldLens is computer vision running in a phone browser. Real-time AR overlays and hands-free glasses are a long-term roadmap idea, not the product you would use this week.
